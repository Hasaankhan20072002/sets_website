School of Emerging Technologies & Systems

SETS is a national research institution focused on generating knowledge and delivering innovative solutions in critical technology areas such as defence, aerospace, information security, AI, quantum tech, and space. Positioned at the intersection of science, engineering, and national security, it addresses high-impact, mission-driven challenges.
Unlike traditional teaching universities, SETS is dedicated to applied research, rapid prototyping, and translational development in technologies of national importance. Its network of specialized Advanced Research & Innovation Centers (ARIC) supports interdisciplinary collaboration to nurture innovation leadership, build enduring scientific capacity, and ensure national technological self-reliance.

Our Vision, Mission & Role

Vision

To be the nation’s premier institution for high-impact applied research and innovation, advancing national security while fostering research-based innovative solutions development across public and private sectors, and contributing to enduring scientific progress and technological ascendancy.

Mission

To create new knowledge and lead strategic, high-impact research and innovation by fostering interdisciplinary collaboration, advancing indigenous technology development, and accelerating the transition of scientific breakthroughs into operational capabilities — thereby strengthening national security, ensuring self-reliance, and achieving technological leadership.

Role

SETS serves as the nation’s flagship institution for advanced systems research, high-impact engineering innovation, and multidisciplinary technology development, anchored around specialized Centres of Advanced Research & Innovation. Through its integrated ecosystem — spanning digital signal processing, adaptive networking, advanced RF systems, spectrum operations, optronics, cybersecurity, quantum technologies, unmanned platforms, super & hypersonics, and space systems — SETS acts as a national catalyst for scientific ascendancy, strategic technological independence, and operational superiority. By connecting deep-tech research with mission-aligned development pathways, SETS ensures that innovations originating in individual Centres combine into coherent, interoperable, future-ready capabilities.

Our Core Tasks

To deliver on its role and fulfil the national Vision and Mission, SETS performs the following core tasks, each inherently infused with the unique strengths of its research Centres:

  1. Lead Strategic, Mission-Driven Research Across Core Technology Domains

    • Advance high-impact research in digital communications, RF systems, networking, optronics, cybersecurity, quantum technologies, unmanned systems, super & hypersonics, Edge AI, and space innovations.
    • Ensure each Centre operates as a knowledge engine driving breakthroughs in its specialized domain.
    • Generate foundational technologies underpinning national security, defence modernization, and scientific competitiveness.

  2. Develop Integrated, Multi-Domain Technological Capabilities

    • Combine outputs from individual Centres into cohesive system-of-systems solutions (e.g., autonomous platforms enabled by quantum-secure communications, AI-enhanced waveforms, resilient networks, and precision RF systems).
    • Ensure cross-Centre interoperability through shared architectures, compatible standards, and aligned R&D objectives.
    • Promote co-development of advanced platforms where communications, sensing, networking, and autonomy converge.

  3. Drive Indigenous Technology Development & Self-Reliance

    • Translate Centre-level research into indigenous products and solutions, including DSP algorithms, MIMO hardware, resilient networking stacks, cyber defence frameworks, and spectrum operations tooling.
    • Develop prototypes, reference designs, testbeds, and validation frameworks aligned with national requirements.
    • Reduce technological dependency in critical domains by building locally scalable, secure, and export-viable capabilities.

  4. Accelerate Innovation to Operational Deployment

    • Establish pathways for each Centre’s research to mature into field-ready technologies for defence, government, and industry.
    • Enable experimentation, rapid prototyping, and test & evaluation through integrated laboratories, simulation ecosystems, and real-world test ranges.
    • Ensure operational users benefit from innovations in waveforms, platforms, spectrum dominance tools, networking solutions, edge AI, quantum systems, and satellite technologies.

  5. Foster Interdisciplinary Collaboration Across Centres

    • Promote cross-pollination of research such as quantum-enhanced secure networking, AI-driven RF sensing, cyber-resilient UAS, and spectrum-aware space communications.
    • Create joint task forces, shared programs, and integrated problem-solving cells across Centres.
    • Reinforce the philosophy that no Centre stands alone — all contribute to a unified national technological strategy.

  6. Build Research Capacity and Train Future Technical Leaders

    • Use each Centre as a talent magnet and training ground for advanced researchers, engineers, and innovators.
    • Offer mentorship, specialized training tracks, interdisciplinary project exposure, and applied research pathways.
    • Cultivate specialists in key domains including:
    • - Autonomous systems development
    • - Super & hypersonics
    • - Advanced DSP algorithms & waveform research
    • - RF & electromagnetic systems
    • - Adaptive networking and tactical communications
    • - Optronics
    • - Cyber and digital infrastructure security
    • - Quantum engineering
    • - Space technologies and mission systems
    • - Edge AI

  7. Provide Strategic Foresight, Technology Advisory & National Guidance

    • Generate Centre-informed foresight studies on emerging threats, disruptive technologies, and future warfare and technology paradigms.
    • Support government and industry with expert advisory on spectrum policy, critical infrastructure security, quantum readiness, UAS governance, and space technology development.
    • Inform national R&D priorities based on insights from the Centres’ research trajectories.

  8. Create Lasting National Impact Through Knowledge & Innovation Dissemination

    • Publish high-value scientific research, technical whitepapers, and strategic studies from the Centres.
    • Engage with academia, startups, and industry to leverage Centre capabilities for broader national development.
    • Drive national competitiveness through innovations in communications, sensing, autonomy, power-efficient systems, precision technologies, and space applications.
